A Visual Reading.
Three diagramsScroll to reveal
Sometimes a passage is best understood not by parsing, but by seeing it whole.
Three drawings, made by hand, that try to show the architecture of the Sermon on the Mount. A mountain. A crowd. A kingdom turned on its head. Read them like a friend's notebook left open on a table.
Fig. IThe Setting

The Mountain.

Jesus climbs away from the crowd before turning to teach them. The geography is the theology. Authority comes from above, but only after the climb.

Matthew V. 1Fig. I
Fig. IIThe Inversion

The Reversal.

Every Beatitude is a quiet rebellion. The people the world ranks last are placed first. The pyramid does not collapse. It is turned over.

Matthew V. 3 to 12Fig. II
Fig. IIIThe Promise

The Inheritance.

Each Beatitude is paired with a promise. The geography of the kingdom is plotted not by territory but by what is given back, in time, to the empty handed.

On the mercy
of small mornings.

May 14, 2026 · Psalm CXXI. 1 to 8 · 5 min read

I lifted my eyes and there was nothing dramatic. No mountain, no thunder. Only the slow pale of dawn through the kitchen window, the kettle whining its single note, and the cat asleep on the chair I had meant to claim. And yet, the verse held. He neither slumbers nor sleeps. Something kept watch in me while I forgot to.

My help comes from the maker of heaven and earth, who has not yet stopped attending to the small mornings.
